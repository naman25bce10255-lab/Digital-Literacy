Digital Security & Awareness

1 Audit Your App Permissions: Periodically check your smartphone settings to ensure apps (espicially flashlight or calculator apps) don't have unncessory access to your contacts, microphone, or location.

2 Use 'Incognito' on Public Terminals: If you must use a library or cyber-cafe computer, always use Private/Incognito mode and never select "Remember Me" for passwords.

3 Secure Your Social Media: Enable Two-Factor Authrntication (2FA) on Instagram, WhatsApp, and LinkedIn using an authenticator app 
rather than just SMS to prevent account takeover. 

4 Verify Educatinal Links: Before clicking on"Scholarship " or "Exam Result" links sent via WhatsApp groups, verify theURL. Official Indian goverenmentsites always end in .gov.in or ,nic.in.

Financial & UPI Safety (India-Specific)

5 The "PIN is for Paying" Ruke: Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone sends you a "Collect Request" claiming it,s a refund or a prize, decline it immediately.

6 Verify the 'VPA'(Virtual Payment Address): Before hitting 'Pay" on a QR code scan at a local vendor or online, always double-check thr name that pops up on the confimation screen to ensure it matches the intented recipient.

Communication & Privacy 

7 Avoid "Free" Public Wi-Fi for Banking: Refrain from logging into bank accounts or making purchases while connected to open campus or railway station Wi-Fi, Use your mobile data for sensitive transactions. 

8 Report 'Digital Arrest' Scams: If you receive a video call from someone claiming to be CBI, Police, or Narcotics Bureau threatening you with "Digital Arrest," hang up immediately. Indian law enforcement doest not conduct arrests or legal precedings over Skype or WhatsApp.
